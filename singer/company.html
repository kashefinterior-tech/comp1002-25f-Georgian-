About The Singer Manufacturing Company

The Singer Manufacturing Company has been making sewing machines since the 1800s. Over time, Singer became a familiar name in a lot of homes, from professional dressmakers to people just hemming their own jeans.

What We’re Trying to Do

Our main goal is simple: create sewing machines that normal people can actually use, that last a long time, and don’t feel scary or complicated.

Why People Like Singer

  • Long history of making sewing machines (not just a random brand)
  • Machines are usually tough, fixable and made to last for years
  • Supports both serious sewing people and beginners
  • Classic designs that still feel nice to use decades later

How the Featherweight Fits Into the Story

The Featherweight 221 is one of the smaller machines in the Singer family. It was made to be light, portable and cute, but still strong enough to actually do work.

For a lot of people, the Featherweight feels like part sewing machine, part little collectible. It shows how Singer tried to mix technology, design and everyday life in one tiny black machine.
